Observe that the set of points (m,p) behaves as an attractor for the Collatz sequence.

This set is organized in layers starting at p=0. Notice that the density is highest near the axes.

Notice also the clustering of these layers and the gap above the first one.

m is an odd number and p is a non-negative integer representing how many times a Collatz term is divided before it becomes odd

This suggests that in order to prove Collatzs it is enough to focus on a proof for the even numbers.

Indeed, suppose that we have proved Collatz up to an odd number q = 2k+1, and let's find the truth for the next one.

The next number is 2k + 1 + 1 = 2k + 2 = 2(k+1)

Applying the rule for even numbers we get k+1 by the next iteration.

But k + 1 is strictly less than 2k + 1, wich is our hypothesis

Q.E.D
